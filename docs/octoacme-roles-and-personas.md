# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises...

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user-centered design practices, conduct research, and validate that delivered solutions meet user needs and usability standards. They collaborate with product and engineering teams to ensure the user experience is intuitive and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design user interfaces, flows, and interactions
- Contribute to requirements and acceptance criteria from a usability perspective
- Validate user experience during delivery through testing and feedback
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design

### Goals
- Ensure user-centered design drives product decisions
- Maximize usability and user satisfaction
- Reduce support burden through intuitive design
- Maintain accessibility standards

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques with engineering and product
- Usability testing sessions and user research findings
- Requirements alignment with developers and product managers
- Accessibility and design system documentation

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and oversee quality assurance strategies, manage test plans, and ensure that delivered features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They work closely with developers and product managers to balance speed and quality.

### Responsibilities
- Define comprehensive test plans and testing strategies
- Oversee test automation and manual QA activities
- Ensure acceptance criteria are satisfied before release
- Identify and document quality issues and risks
- Coach team on quality best practices and testing techniques

### Goals
- Maintain high quality standards and reduce production defects
- Optimize test coverage and automation
- Ensure release readiness and confidence
- Reduce cycle time through efficient testing

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and QA status reports in sprint reviews
- Bug reports and defect triage sessions
- Release readiness assessments
- Quality metrics and trend analysis

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate deployments, manage release schedules, and ensure smooth transitions to production. They work with developers, QA, and project managers to minimize risk and maintain communication throughout the release process.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Ensure rollback and mitigation plans are documented
- Manage pre-release checklists and verification
- Communicate release timelines to stakeholders
- Lead post-deployment verification and incident response if needed

### Goals
- Minimize deployment risk and ensure release success
- Maintain consistent, reliable release processes
- Enable rapid and safe deployments to production
- Coordinate cross-team release activities

### Typical Communication
- Release schedules and deployment checklists
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Post-deployment verification and status updates
- Incident escalation and rollback coordination

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders represent business, executive, or functional interests in a project. They provide requirements, review deliverables, grant approvals, and ensure that project outcomes align with organizational objectives and priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and success criteria
- Review key deliverables and milestones
- Grant approval authority for go/no-go decisions
- Participate in key retrospectives and decision-making meetings
- Communicate project value and outcomes to their organizations

### Goals
- Ensure business value is delivered
- Provide governance and strategic alignment
- Enable timely decision-making through engagement
- Align project outcomes with organizational objectives

### Typical Communication
- Requirement and specification reviews
- Milestone and approval gate meetings
- Status updates and executive summaries
- Post-release or project close-out briefings

---

## Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide deep domain knowledge, validate technical or compliance approaches, and ensure that solutions align with best practices and organizational standards. They serve as consultants and mentors to the broader team.

### Responsibilities
- Supply specialized domain knowledge and expertise
- Validate technical approaches and solution design
- Ensure compliance and adherence to standards
- Review solution feasibility and identify risks
- Mentor team members and share best practices

### Goals
- Ensure solutions leverage best practices and domain expertise
- Reduce technical and compliance risk
- Enable team learning and skill development
- Maintain organizational standards and consistency

### Typical Communication
- Technical design review sessions
- Consultation and mentoring interactions
- Best practice guidance and documentation
- Compliance and standards validation

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning projects, ensure that all relevant personas are represented in the team or consulted appropriately.
